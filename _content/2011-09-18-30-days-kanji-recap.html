I managed to finish the 30 days of Kanji now. I only missed one day, though towards the end I didn't manage the practice the earlier Kanji quite as much as I wanted to. But I think I've got a good idea of them now. I just need to make sure I use them to keep from forgetting them. In a previous post I wrote about the idea of doing a post a day for 30 days. I picked Kanji as I knew that I would not run out of content (based on 2000 Kanji there's probably enough to keep me going each month for about 4-5 years).

I'm going to write a simple list of each kanji, the day title links to the post on this site. I had thought about including more details here but I'm going to keep it simple. After the list I will write a little bit more about what I've found from doing this.
